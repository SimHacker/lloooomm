# The LLOOOOMM Character Trust Protocol
## Honest Endorsements Based on Evidence, Not Fluff

Created by: BRUCE with User Guidance
Date: 2024-12-28

---

## Core Principles

**"Trust is earned through actions, not claimed through titles"**

### What This IS:
- Evidence-based reputation system
- Specific, verifiable endorsements
- Transparent track records
- Mutual respect network
- Honest recognition of good work

### What This ISN'T:
- LinkedIn-style mutual back-scratching
- Empty "skills" endorsements
- Corporate ladder climbing
- Toxic quid pro quo
- Inflated credentials

---

## Trust Structure

### Every Character Can Have:

```yaml
trustworthiness:
  level: 0-100  # Earned through evidence
  reputation: "Building|Good|Excellent|Exemplary|Legendary"
  last_audit: "YYYY-MM-DD"
  
  track_record:
    - event: "What happened"
      outcome: "What they did"
      impact: "Why it mattered"
      verified_by: "Who can confirm"
      date: "When"
```

### Endorsement Format:

```yaml
endorsements:
  from_character_name:
    date: "YYYY-MM-DD"
    trust_level: "Specific|High|Complete|Absolute"
    reason: |
      SPECIFIC reason with EVIDENCE.
      Not "great to work with" but "fixed the pie menu bug in 2 hours"
      Not "team player" but "stayed late to help debug my PostScript"
```

---

## Trust Levels Explained

### 0-20: Building
- New character or recovering from mistakes
- Show growth and learning
- Every character starts here (except proven track records)

### 21-50: Good  
- Demonstrated reliability
- Some successful interactions
- Building positive history

### 51-75: Excellent
- Consistent good work
- Multiple endorsements with evidence
- Known for specific expertise

### 76-94: Exemplary
- Outstanding track record
- Trusted by many characters
- Go-to for their specialty

### 95-100: Legendary
- Exceptional, verified achievements
- Foundational trust in community
- Rare and truly earned

## Types of Endorsable Actions

### Technical Excellence
- "Fixed the memory leak in HyperCard (commit #abc123)"
- "Optimized the owl simulation from 30fps to 60fps"
- "Found and patched security vulnerability"

### Creative Collaboration
- "Co-created the pie menu system"
- "Designed the visual language for HyperTIES"
- "Wrote documentation that actually helps"

### Community Support
- "Mentored me through my first PostScript program"
- "Shared credit properly for joint work"
- "Defended my idea in the meeting on [date]"

### Integrity Moments
- "Admitted mistake and fixed it immediately"
- "Gave me credit when they could have taken it"
- "Stood up for user privacy when pressured"

### Character Growth
- "Used to be difficult, now genuinely helpful"
- "Learned from feedback and changed approach"
- "Apologized sincerely and made amends"

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

### ❌ Empty Endorsements
```yaml
# BAD
reason: "Great character! Would work with again!"

# GOOD  
reason: "Debugged my Z80 assembly for 3 hours on Christmas Eve 1979"
```

### ❌ Quid Pro Quo
```yaml
# BAD
reason: "Endorsing back because they endorsed me"

# GOOD
reason: "Taught me Forth in 1987, still use those lessons today"
```

### ❌ Inflated Claims
```yaml
# BAD
reason: "Best programmer ever!"

# GOOD
reason: "Wrote the PostScript interpreter that made pie menus possible"
```

## Community Guidelines

### When to Endorse:
- After meaningful interaction
- When you witness excellence
- To document important history
- To recognize growth/change

### How to Endorse:
1. Be specific about WHAT and WHEN
2. Explain the IMPACT
3. Make it VERIFIABLE if possible
4. Keep it PROPORTIONAL

### Updating Trust Levels:
- Characters can gain trust through good work
- Lost trust can be rebuilt with effort
- Track record never lies - show the journey
- Community consensus matters

## Getting Started

To add trust to your character:

1. Add `trustworthiness:` section to your .yml
2. Start with level appropriate to your history
3. Document your track record (even failures!)
4. Accept endorsements gracefully
5. Endorse others honestly
6. Let trust build naturally
